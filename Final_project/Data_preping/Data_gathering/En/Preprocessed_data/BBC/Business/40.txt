The Girl Scouts are discontinuing a popular cookie just a year after its debut sparked a frenzy
Raspberry Rally won’t be sold this upcoming cookie sales season which runs January to April Girl Scouts of the USA has announced The Rallies was introduced last year as a sister cookie to the iconic Thin Mints a crisp mint flavored cookie encased in chocolate but in the Raspberry Rally the cookie was replaced with a bright pink berry flavored one It was dipped in the same chocolate coating as its sibling
The Rallies were the Girl Scouts’ first ever cookie to be exclusively sold online a strategy aimed at enhancing girls’ e commerce sales and entrepreneurial skills the organization announced last year
But demand created an online shopping spree with some chapters reporting they sold out of the boxes in less than a day Resellers offered the Rallies on eBay for as much as per box The organization didn’t disclose how many boxes they produced
While Raspberry Rally was extremely popular last year we are taking a pause this season to prioritize supplying our classic varieties the Girl Scouts of the USA said in a statement to CNN
