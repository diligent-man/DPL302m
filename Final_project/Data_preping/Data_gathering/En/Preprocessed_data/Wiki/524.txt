The Third Book of Enoch Hebrew ספר חנוך לר׳ ישמעאל כ׳׳ג‬‬ abbreviated as Enoch is a Biblical apocryphal book in Hebrew Enoch purports to have been written in the nd century but its origins can only be traced to the th century Other names for Enoch include The Book of the Palaces The Book of Rabbi Ishmael the High Priest and The Revelation of Metatron
Most commonly the Book of Enoch refers to Enoch which survived completely only in Ge'ez There is also a Second Book of Enoch which has survived only in Old Slavonic although Coptic fragments were also identified in
Modern scholars describe this book as pseudepigraphal as it says it is written by Rabbi Ishmael who became a high priest after visions of ascension to Heaven This has been taken as referring to Rabbi Ishmael a rd generation Tanna and a leading figure of Merkabah mysticism However this Ishmael lived after the Siege of Jerusalem AD and the destruction of the Second Temple in AD He thus could not have been a High Priest of Israel An alternative identification would be the earlier Tanna Ishmael ben Elisha who lived through the Siege of Jerusalem
The name Sefer Hekhalot Hekhalot meaning palaces or temples along with its proposed author places this book as a member of Hekalot or Merkabah mysticism Its contents suggest that Enoch's contents and ideas are newer than those shown in other Merkabah texts The book does not contain Merkabah hymns it has a unique layout and adjuration All these facts make Enoch unique not just among Merkabah writings but also within the writings of Enoch
Enoch contains a number of Greek and Latin words The book appears to have been originally written in Hebrew There are a number of indications suggesting that the writers of Enoch had knowledge of and most likely read Enoch
