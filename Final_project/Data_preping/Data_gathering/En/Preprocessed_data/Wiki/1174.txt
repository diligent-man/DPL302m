South Korean standard language or Pyojuneo Korean Hanja is the South Korean standard version of the Korean language It is based on the Seoul dialect although various words are borrowed from other regional dialects It uses the Hangul alphabet created in December by Joseon era king Sejong the Great Unlike North Korean standard language Munhwa South Korean standard language includes many loan words from Chinese as well as some from English and from other European languages
When Korea was under Japanese rule the use of the Korean language was regulated by the Japanese government To counter the influence of the Japanese authorities the Korean Language Society began collecting dialect data from all over Korea and later created their own standard version of Korean Pyojuneo with the release of their book Unification of Korean Spellings in
