Chadlington is a village and civil parish in the Evenlode Valley about miles km south of Chipping Norton Oxfordshire The village has five neighbourhoods Brookend Eastend Greenend Millend and Westend
There is a bowl barrow about miles km west of the village Bowl barrows range from late Neolithic to late Bronze Age ie to BC The barrow is a scheduled monument Knollbury is a hill fort mile km northwest of the village It is a scheduled monument
Chadlington village existed by the time of Domesday Book in and may be named after Saint Chad The present Chadlington Manor House was built in the th century and remodelled in about It is a Grade II listed building Lower Court farmhouse was built in about as the manor house for Westend It was altered in the mid to late th century and remodelled in the th The house has a Stonesfield Slate roof It is a Grade II listed building
The Church of England parish church of Saint Nicholas was originally Norman and the blocked head of a Norman window above the north arcade show that the building had a clerestory in Norman times In the th century Early English Gothic north and south aisles were added with four bay arcades linking the aisles with the nave Both aisles still retain some lancet windows from this period The bell tower was built early in the th century in the Decorated Gothic style A chapel was added at the east end of the north aisle The chapel's east and north windows in the Transitional style between Decorated and Perpendicular Gothic date it to the chancel about the middle of the th century Later pure Perpendicular Gothic additions include the windows and north door in the north aisle the present clerestory and nave roof and the chancel arch In the Gothic Revival architect Charles Buckeridge completely rebuilt the chancel St Nicholas' church is a Grade II listed building
The tower has a ring of six bells Abraham I Rudhall of Gloucester cast the second and third bells in William Taylor of Loughborough cast the fifth and tenor bells in at the bellfoundry he then had in Oxford Thomas Bond of Burford cast the fourth bell in The Whitechapel Bell Foundry cast the present tenor bell in There is also a Sanctus bell that Thomas Bond cast in In the Church of England Benefice of Ascott under Wychwood Chadlington and Spelsbury merged with that of Enstone and Heythrop to form the Chase Benefice The Parsonage was designed by William Wilkinson and built in It is now Chadlington House
Chadlington Baptist chapel was built in It is now a private house
Chadlington has a Methodist church It is a member of Chipping Norton and Stow on the Wold Methodist Circuit
Chadlington used to have three public houses the Malt Shovel the Tite Inn and the Sandys Arms The Tite Inn was closed between and when it reopened with new owners and the Sandys Arms on Bull Hill closed some years earlier Sir Henry Rawlinson and his brother Canon George Rawlinson were born in Chadlington Ivan Cameron son of former Conservative Party Leader and Prime Minister David Cameron is buried in Chadlington
Chadlington Football Club play in the Witney and District League It currently has two teams in Divisions One and Four Chadlington Cricket Club play in the Oxfordshire Cricket Association League Both clubs are based at Chadlington Sports and Social Club Chadlington also has a primary school and a playgroup An annual fun run is held along Coldron Brook The Great Brook Run Proceeds of the race which starts and ends at The Tite Inn go to the parish primary school Former Witney MP and Prime Minister David Cameron has taken part in the event
