hassan al attar was appointed sheikh of al azhar in and became one of the earliest reformist clerics in ottoman egypt
he was a forerunner of egypt s national revival and his legacy was a generation of egyptian modernists like his disciple rifa al tahtawi
he advocated the introduction of sciences such as logic and modern astronomy and wrote the first modern history of mohammed s tribe the quraish
he was to suffer greatly for his modernizing beliefs
his first contact with foreign non muslim knowledge came during the french occupation of egypt
fearing for his safety after the french withdrawal he left cairo for istanbul

he was the first director of the new medical college defending the necessity of corpse dissection which he had observed in the cairo veterinary college against the non experimental theoretical teachings of eleventh century avicenna discarded centuries ago in christian europe
while he was a successful lecturer at al azhar university his time there was marked by continual conflict with un westernized ulemas leading him at times to conduct classes in his home
the tensions only became worse with his appointment as rector
he died within four years
