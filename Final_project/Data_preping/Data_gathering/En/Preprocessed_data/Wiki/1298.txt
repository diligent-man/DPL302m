There is a Stone Age barrow in the town and evidence of Roman settlement in the nd and rd centuries however the town was established by the Saxons The church of St Mary the Virgin has a Saxon cross shaft dating from the th century
The name Gillingham was used for the town in its th century Saxon charter and also in an entry for in the annals as the location of a battle between Edmund Ironside and the Vikings In the Domesday Book in it is recorded as Gelingeham and later spellings include Gellingeham in Gyllingeham in and Gilingeham in The name derives from a personal name plus the Old English inga and h m and means a homestead of the family or followers of a man called Gylla
