Johannes Antonius Lammers born June is a racing driver and team principal from the Netherlands
In Lammers made his debut in Formula One driving for Shadow and moved to ATS for He moved to Ensign midway through the season but rejoined ATS for four races in He joined Theodore for Ten years later he returned to Formula One for the final two races of the season
He won the Hours of Le Mans in and later participated in the race with his own team Racing for Holland He was also the seatholder of the Dutch A Grand Prix team
Lammers grew up as a kid washing cars at a sliding school in Zandvoort As a year old he started to show customers how to slide safely encouraged by sliding school owner and racing driver Rob Slotemaker who quickly recognized Lammers's talent When he was years old he started his first race at the local racing track In the following years he starred in Dutch touring car and Formula Ford races before moving up to European Formula in He did not get good results with his Hawke and for his second season he joined Racing Team Holland run by Alan Docking
The other drivers of the team were fellow future Formula driver Huub Rothengatter and later Indy winner Arie Luyendijk It was a successful move as Lammers won the European Formula Championship after a close battle with Swede Anders Olofsson
Lammers began his Formula One career with Shadow in with Elio de Angelis as his teammate He did not have a great debut as de Angelis was the team's number one driver His best result was a ninth place in Canada
In he moved to Günther Schmidt's ATS team In the first three races he failed to qualify but in Long Beach he qualified a very strong fourth This would be the highlight of his F career Later that year he moved to the Ensign Team but there he only managed to qualify three times In he returned to ATS but only raced the first four races of the year For he moved to Theodore but his season was something of a disaster He did manage to start in his home race He scored no championship points during his F career
In and Lammers made CART World Series starts for four different teams His best finish was fifth at Laguna Seca Raceway in for Forsythe Racing He was entered and practiced for the Indianapolis in a Mike Curb entry but did not make a qualifying attempt He finished th and nd in points in his two seasons respectively After that he turned his attention to sports car racing
In Lammers together with Andy Wallace and Johnny Dumfries won the Le Mans with the Silk Cut JaguarTWR team Lammers drove out of the hours and beat the Porsche team who had remained unbeaten since Lammers and the team managed to finish first despite a broken gearbox It was the first victory for Jaguar since
For this feat Jan Lammers received the title Honorary Member of the BRDC a title rarely awarded to non British residents Enzo Ferrari and Juan Manuel Fangio have also received the award
In Lammers won the Hours of Daytona driving a Jaguar XJR along with Davy Jones and Andy Wallace
Lammers returned to Le Mans in with Hope Racing in the LMP category
In Lammers made a two race comeback in Formula One for March in Japan and Australia These races marked his first Formula One appearance since which is the longest gap between successive Grands Prix in the history of Formula One He was signed for the team in alongside French driver Jean Marc Gounon but the team went bankrupt before the season started
In Lammers teamed up with TWR again to race in the British Touring Car Championship BTCC driving the Volvo estate
In Jan Lammers started his own Sportcar team Racing for Holland He raced in the FIA Sportscar Championship together with his teammate and pupil Val Hillebrand In and he earned the FIA Awards for his world championships in the Sportscar series
Since Jan Lammers has run the Dutch A Grandprix team for the A Grand Prix series with drivers including Jos Verstappen and Jeroen Bleekemolen and Renger van der Zande
Lammers participated in all three Grand Prix Masters events A seventh place in the Qatar race was his best result
Lammers's son René Lammers is also a racing driver currently competing in karting
