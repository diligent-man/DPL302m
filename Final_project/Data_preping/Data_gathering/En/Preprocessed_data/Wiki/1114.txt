Tibetan Empire had nine high ministers in court
The highest one was gung lön chen po གུང་བློན་ཆེན་པོ lön chen བློན་ཆེན for short which could be translated into English as Chief Minister or Prime minister
The first Lönchen was appointed during Detrul Namshungtsen's reign
It was abolished before the Era of Fragmentation
