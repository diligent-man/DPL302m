The Eight Great Yak a Generals Chinese B d Y ch D ji ng or simply the Eight Yak a Generals are guardian deities in Buddhism They are retainers of Vai rava a guardian of the north and king of the yak as
In East Asia they are also variously known as the Eight Great Yak as the Eight Great Heavenly Kings and the Eight Brothers of Vai rava a
The term yak ar k asa has been used as a general term to denote the many classes of spirits in Indian mythology combining the words yak a and r k asa
According to Buddhist mythology Vai rava a is the chief of these beings and long ago dwelt together with them in the realm of darkness When Vai rava a converted to Buddhism the many demonic spirits under his jurisdiction likewise assumed the role of devotees to the Buddha Originally malevolent beings their conversion led to their deification as benevolent guardian deities
Among the many yak as under Vai rava a's rule the Eight Great Yak a Generals are ranked at the top of the hierarchy Always at Vai rava a's command these deities command yak as that serve their king and are said to protect those who venerate them A similar list may be found among the Twenty Eight Great Yak a Generals
