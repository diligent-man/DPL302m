The Honor Rolls of Baseball were established in by the National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum's Permanent Committee to establish as a second level of induction designed to recognize non playing contributors The committee designed the Honor Rolls to commemorate managers executives umpires and sportswriters as an addition to their regular vote of old time players Though sportswriter Henry Chadwick was elected in the Hall had not devised a plan to extend recognition to these contributors and this was the first attempt
On April the Permanent Committee voted to induct players into the Baseball Hall of Fame along with non players into the Honor Rolls separated into their respective category This second tier list consisted of five managers umpires executives and sportswriters
