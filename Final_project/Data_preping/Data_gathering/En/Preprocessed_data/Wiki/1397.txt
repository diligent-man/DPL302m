Herbert Smith Gunn April March was a British newspaper editor
Born in Gravesend Bert Gunn worked as a reporter for the Kent Messenger and then the Straits Times in Singapore He returned to the UK to work at the Manchester Evening News then the London Evening News and the Evening Standard He also married and had two sons Thom Gunn later a poet and Ander Gunn later a photographer
In Gunn became the first northern editor of the Daily Express then in became managing editor He wrote the headline It's That Man Again referring to Hitler later to become the title of a popular radio show
Gunn became editor of the Evening Standard in but owner Lord Beaverbrook disagreed with his plans to adopt a more populist approach and Gunn left in Gunn also revealed that Labour Party MP Garry Allighan was leaking stories to the newspaper following which Allighan resigned
In Gunn joined the Daily Sketch as its editor and doubled its circulation in six years In he was the President of the Institute of Journalists He moved to edit the Sunday Dispatch in but this was merged with the Sunday Express in and Gunn resigned from Associated Newspapers in
