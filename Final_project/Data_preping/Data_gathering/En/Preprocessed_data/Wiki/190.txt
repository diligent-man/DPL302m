Lamont Herbert Dozier ləˈmɒnt ˈdoʊʒər born June is an American singer songwriter and record producer born in Detroit Michigan United States He has co written and produced US Billboard number hits and number ones in the UK
Dozier was a member of Holland Dozier Holland the songwriting and production team responsible for much of the Motown sound and numerous hit records by artists such as Martha and the Vandellas The Supremes The Four Tops and The Isley Brothers Along with Brian Holland Dozier served as the team's musical arranger and producer while Eddie Holland concentrated mainly on lyrics and vocal production
Along with the Holland Brothers Dozier followed his work for Motown Records as founder and owner of Invictus Records and Hot Wax Records producing top charting hits for acts Freda Payne Honey Cone Chairmen of the Board and Proof Aged in Soul
Dozier recorded a few unsuccessful records for various Detroit labels before the trio started working together as a writing and production team for Motown in They first made their mark the following year with Martha and The Vandellas' early hits including Come and Get These Memories number RB Heatwave number RB number pop and Quicksand number pop
In Where Did Our Love Go became the first of ten number pop hits which Holland Dozier Holland would write and produce for the Supremes over the next three years or so After Holland Dozier Holland left Motown in to form the Invictus and Hot Wax labels Dozier began recording as an artist on their labels The most successful song was Why Can't We Be Lovers number Billboard RB Dozier departed from Holland Dozier Holland in and was replaced by new arranger producer Harold Beatty
Dozier went on to record a number of albums as a performer in his own right also writing much of the material The album Peddlin' Music on the Side Warner Bros Records contained Going Back to My Roots which was later recorded by Odyssey The earlier Black Bach ABC Records featured the country flavored All Cried Out RB No pop
He had his biggest hit with 's Trying to Hold on to My Woman ABC which reached No on the pop chart and No on the RB chart For the second season of the TV sitcom That's My Mama ABC Dozier wrote and sang the theme song replacing the first season's instrumental only theme music In he scored a beach music hit with Cool Me Out
Dozier had another number hit as a songwriter in the s joining with Phil Collins to write the song Two Hearts for the movie soundtrack for Buster Two Hearts received a Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song tying with Let the River Run from Working Girl by Carly Simon an Academy Award nomination for Best Original Song and a Grammy Award for Best Song Written Specifically for a Motion Picture or Television Collins and Dozier also co wrote Loco in Acapulco for The Four Tops which is also featured on the Buster Soundtrack
In Essex England born singer Alison Moyet scored a US top hit with the Dozier penned Invisible Three years later Dozier cowrote Infidelity and Suffer with Simply Red frontman Mick Hucknall for the British pop soul band's second album Men and Women In they teamed again to write You've Got It and Turn It Up for Simply Red's follow up LP A New Flame
In Lamont Dozier composed alone a song for the soundtrack of another film the song Without You which was recorded as a duet by the RB singers Peabo Bryson and Regina Belle to be the love theme from the comedy film Leonard Part released the same year The film starred Bill Cosby in the lead role The song was released as a single and entered the United States and United Kingdom music charts peaked at No on the Adult Contemporary Tracks No on the RB chart No on the UK Singles and No on the Billboard Hot Without You was also recorded for the Peabo Bryson album Positive released in
Without You also received two adaptations the first was in Portuguese and the second was in Spanish in and respectively Both adaptations received the title Amor Dividido and were recorded by the Brazilian singer Rosana Amor Dividido in Spanish is a duet by Rosana with the Spanish singer Emmanuel
Dozier and the Holland brothers were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in
In he worked on the music for the musical stage version of the movie First Wives Club He is also taught credit courses on popular music as an Artist in Residence Professor on the faculty at the University of Southern California Thornton School of Music in
Lamont Dozier lives in Encino California with his wife They have three children
