It is an interchange between National Rail between central London and Dartford north Kent and the Docklands Light Railway DLR between Lewisham to the south and Docklands and the City of London
It is the nearest National Rail station to the centre of Greenwich but Cutty Sark for Maritime Greenwich DLR station is closer to the town centre and its tourist attractions

Northwards the DLR goes into a tunnel through Cutty Sark station and under the River Thames to the Isle of Dogs in the opposite direction it rises on a concrete viaduct to follow the River Ravensbourne upstream to Deptford Bridge and Lewisham

The National Rail line is one of London's oldest the London and Greenwich Railway is reputed to be the world's first suburban railway
It was designed by former army engineer George Landmann and promoted by entrepreneur George Walter
A massive brick viaduct with arches was built to a station in Spa Road Bermondsey and later to London Bridge
The line opened on February from Deptford and on December from a temporary station in Greenwich
Greenwich's handsome station building was designed by George Smith and opened in making it one of the oldest station buildings in the world
The South Eastern Railway SER leased the Greenwich branch from January
The South Eastern and Chatham Railway was formed on January and as such took over operation of the station
