Church of Michael the Archangel Russian Azerbaijani Mixayil Arxangel kils si or Flotskaya Church of the Fleet is a Russian Orthodox church in central Baku Azerbaijan on Zargarpalan street The church is dedicated to St Michael The church is serviced by Bishop Mefodi Efendiyev three priests and one deacon
The church used to be among the military churches and belonged to the Caspian Flotilla and was therefore called Flotskaya Although the exact date of the church's establishment is unknown the approximate date is as described in writings of the Kavkaz Caucasus newspaper published in The church was built by the Caspian fleet officers in honor of the chief of the navy Alexander Nikolayevich Tsesarevich who along with Russian Emperor Nikolay I visited Baku in The building follows the Pskov construction style and the prayer room looks like a ship deck
On August by orders of Alexander II the Flotskaya Church was transferred to the Military Office for needs of the Salyan Reserve Regiment which arrived from Lenkoran in In the management of the church was transferred to the Caspian Eparchy Office From November to November the building underwent renovation works at a cost of rubles In the church was renamed to Polkovoya of Salyan Regiment It was eventually shut down in and turned into a communal house during the Soviet rule of Azerbaijan but was later re opened as a church in following World War II after the role of religion in bringing victory was recognized by the Soviet authorities
