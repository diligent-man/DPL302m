Poush Mela Bengali is an annual fair and festival that takes place in Santiniketan in Birbhum District in the Indian state of West Bengal marking the harvest season Commencing on the th day of the month of Poush the fair officially lasts for three days although vendors may stay until the month end as per the university regulations From onwards the fair lasted for six days The key characteristic of this fair include live performances of Bengali folk music such as baul kirtan and Kobigan
Devendranath Tagore with twenty followers accepted the Brahmo creed from Ram Chandra Vidyabagish on December Poush according to the Bengali calendar This was the basis of Poush Utsav the Festival of Poush at Santiniketan
A Brahma mandir was established at Santiniketan on December Poush according to the Bengali calendar A small fair was organised in in connection with the establishment anniversary of the Brahma mandir in the ground opposite the mandir What started as a small homely Poush Mela now attracts attention of not only the people of Birbhum district but tourists from all around
From onwards the Poush Mela has been organized every year However it has been put on halt for three times due to the Bengal Famine of Direct Action Day of and the COVID pandemic
In earlier days the mela fair was held in the ground on the north side of Brahma mandir also referred to as glass temple On that day a firework display was held in earlier days after evening prayers As the mela increased in size it was shifted to the field in Purbapalli
Poush Utsav is inaugurated on Poush around December At dawn Santiniketan wakes up to the soft music of shehnai The first to enter the scenario is the Vaitalik group who go round the ashrama hermitage singing songs It is followed by a prayer meeting at Chhatimtala Then the entire congregation moves on to Uttarayan singing songs
Poush Mela is characterized by the live performances of Bengali folk music especially the baul music It includes folk songs dances and tribal sports This fair offers a perfect insight of the true heritage of the state The students of Santiniketan present their magnificent performance and make this festival more enjoyable and glamorous Each day of this festival is filled with different activities The last day of this fair is devoted to those who are related to Santiniketan
