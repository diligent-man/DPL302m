When Morton retired he surprised many who considered him at the peak of his political power However he opposed the Vietnam War despite being criticized by Rep William Cowger from Kentucky Also he was both depressed by the urban violence after the April assassination of Martin Luther King Jr and that of Robert F Kennedy a few weeks later and disappointed in his party's failure to address the broader social issues He also ultimately counseled then President Lyndon Johnson to decline to seek re election and he supported the unsuccessful presidential candidacy of Gov Nelson Rockefeller of New York
Morton is interviewed in the documentary film In the Year of the Pig and another interview is available through the Lyndon Baines Johnson Presidential Library
After his retirement from the U S Senate Morton served as vice chairman of Liberty National Bank in Louisville president of the American Horse Council and chairman of the board of Churchill Downs and he served as one of the directors of the University of Louisville Pillsbury Company Pittston Company Louisville Board of Trade Texas Gas Company R J Reynolds Company and the Ohio Valley Assembly
