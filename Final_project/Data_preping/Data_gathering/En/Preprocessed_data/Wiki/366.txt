babbel is a premium subscription based language learning app for web ios and android
babbel currently offers different languages from seven display languages german english us uk french spanish brazilian portuguese italian and swedish
babbel s original learning content is developed in house by a team of over educators and linguists
there are beginner intermediate and grammar courses vocabulary lessons as well as courses with tongue twisters idioms colloquialisms and sayings
courses for a given language may be aimed at a specific audience for instance english may be learned as pr english or marketing english
in august babbel announced that it had partnered with cambridge english language assessment to create a low cost online english test
the test assesses beginning and intermediate students reading and listening skills up to level b and above of the common european framework of reference for languages
every test features about questions from a bank of hundreds of options which like babbel s lesson content reflect real life communicative situations including recordings of radio broadcasts and conversations for listening tasks
juliet wilson director of assessment at cambridge english explained to professionals in international education news that
until now it s been difficult for online learners to know whether they are really learning the right skills or to demonstrate their real level going on to say that the babbel english test would give learners reliable evidence of their progress and a certificate of achievement that demonstrates what they have learned
the word babbel is the imperative mood of babbeln which means to chat in the hessian dialect of german and in dutch
it is also a pun on the biblical tower of babel a gigantic ziggurat whose construction was interrupted when the workers languages were made mutually unintelligible by god
douglas adams used the same idea in his hitchhiker s guide to the galaxy to name the babel fish a symbiotic fish that serves as a universal translator
babbel is also a homophone and anagram of the english verb babble
