erdington abbey church grid reference sp on sutton road erdington birmingham england is the more usual name of the grade ii listed church of saints thomas and edmund of canterbury
it is the church of a roman catholic parish in the archdiocese of birmingham served by the redemptorists
the abbey itself was the adjacent building now highclare school
in father heneage built a chapel in erdington high street on the croft opposite the end of station lane
before this priests from oscott college had said mass in a house on the high street but catholics in erdington are mainly indebted to the rev
daniel h
haigh founder of the church of ss thomas edmund of canterbury
he laid the foundation stone of the new church on may
the church was opened and consecrated by bishop ullathorne on june
the church is an example of the gothic revival
the church was designed by charles hansom who built the steeple of the church ft m high which is also the length of the building
the plate was designed by augustus pugin and made by hardman
in father haigh handed over his church parish and estate of acres m to the benedictine monks from beuron in germany exiled for their faith from their own country during the kulturkampf the anti catholic and anti clerical movement headed by bismarck
the abbey was built on this land next to the church
its building is also grade ii listed
the benedictine monks were later displaced a second time as a result of problems experienced by the predominantly german beuronese congregation during world war i
after world war i during which the community suffered aggravation it was possible for the community to return to their homeland
the parish came under the control of the redemptorist order of priests in and is currently served by fr
elias gweme cssr rector and parish priest fr
francis dickinson cssr fr
isaac davies cssr and fr
royston price cssr
the attached cemetery contains war graves of three soldiers of world war i and two soldiers and four royal air force personnel of world war ii
