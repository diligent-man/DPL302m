The Worshipful Company of Management Consultants is one of the Livery Companies of the City of London
It draws its memberships from practising management consultants and has close links to the Management Consultancies Association and the Institute of Management Consultancy
The Company's motto is 'Change through Wisdom'
It started as a guild in and became a company without livery in
On May the Court of Aldermen granted the company's petition to become the City's th Livery Company
The Company was presented with a royal charter by Prince Edward Earl of Wessex in May
Between and the Company had worked to establish a record of achievement in the fields of education and charity whilst gaining the financial resources required for admission to the livery
The Livery Companies continue a long tradition of supporting protecting and developing their trades and crafts whilst acting in charitable ways within the wider community
They have also traditionally been 'clubs' in which colleagues assemble in fellowship and social intercourse
The Worshipful Company of Management Consultants continues these traditions by undertaking pro bono consultancy for charities including supporting the Centre for Charity Effectiveness which it established in partnership with the Cass Business School of the City University in London and forming close ties with the Sea Cadets
The Company is a member of the Financial Services Group of Livery Companies the other members of which are the Worshipful Companies of Chartered Accountants Actuaries Arbitrators International Bankers Chartered Secretaries and Administrators Insurers Information Technologists City of London Solicitors Marketors Tax Advisers and World Traders
The Company has liverymen senior members and some junior freemen who gather six or seven times a year for formal dinners and more frequently for informal social gatherings
The Company uses the Worshipful Company of Skinners' livery hall in Dowgate for events
A stained glass window designed by Stella Timmins to commemorate the Company was installed at the Guildhall in and was officially recognised by the Lord Mayor of London at a ceremony on October
The Company's Church is St James Garlickhythe
