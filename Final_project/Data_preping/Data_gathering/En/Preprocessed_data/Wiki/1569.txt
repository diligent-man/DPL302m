Evelina Gertrude de Rothschild August December was an English socialite and a member of the Rothschild banking family of England
Evelina de Rothschild was the daughter of Baron Lionel de Rothschild the first openly unconverted Jew to sit in the British House of Commons Her mother was Charlotte von Rothschild a cousin from the Naples branch of the family
On June Evelina married her second cousin Ferdinand James von Rothschild of the Austrian branch of the family Because of her parents' prominent position as one of the wealthiest and most influential families in England guests at her wedding banquet and ball included Benjamin Disraeli the ambassadors from Austria and France and Prince George Duke of Cambridge
Following a lengthy honeymoon across Europe Evelina and her husband settled into a home at Piccadilly in London near her parents She died on December after giving birth to their first child a stillborn son She is buried at the Rothschild Mausoleum in the Jewish Cemetery at West Ham
In her memory her husband built equipped and endowed the Evelina Hospital for Sick Children in Southwark one of the neglected and poorest districts of London Her father Lionel assumed sponsorship in of the first school for girls in Israel which opened in Jerusalem in and renamed it the Evelina de Rothschild School Her mother Charlotte inaugurated the Evelina Prize awarded at Jewish elementary schools and Jews' College
